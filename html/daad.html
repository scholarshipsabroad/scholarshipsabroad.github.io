Research Grants for Doctoral Candidates and Young Scientists and Academics

Research grants to run up to 6 months: Application deadline: 31 October each year. Requirement: Excellent Master's or PhD degree, completed less than six years ago The DAAD will pay an appropriate flat-rate travel allowance, a monthly stipend and a health insurance cover. German language skills are not expected. Online application Research grants to run for 6 - 36 months (full grant scholarship): Application deadline: 31 October each year. Requirement: Excellent Master's degree, completed less than six years ago Field research in the home country is possible for up to 12 months. The grants are open to all academic disciplines. German language skills are not expected. The DAAD will pay an appropriate flat-rate travel allowance, a monthly stipend and a health insurance cover. Funding for field work in the home country not provided by DAAD Online application Grants in accordance with the DAAD Sandwich Model: Application deadline: 31 October each year Requirement: Excellent Master's degree, completed less than six years ago Funding for field work in the home country not provided by DAAD. Only 2 phases of field research (of altogether 12 months) in your home country or “third country” are possible. Residence in Germany is subject to a time limit of 24 months. The Doctorate degree has to be gained in the home country. German language skills are not expected. For the stay in Germany, the DAAD will pay an appropriate flat-rate travel allowance, a monthly stipend and a health insurance cover Online application To do research in Germany, applicants need an admission at a Graduate School in Germany, or the confirmation/ invitation of a German supervisor based at a university or research institute. It often proves difficult to find such a research contact. Therefore, it is advisable to start the search as early as possible!
